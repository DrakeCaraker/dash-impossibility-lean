Attribution instability is not a bug in SHAP — it is a mathematical property of attribution itself, at every level of a model. We prove that no attribution ranking can simultaneously be faithful (reflect the model's attributions), stable (consistent across equivalent models), and complete (rank all components) when interchangeable components exist (the Attribution Impossibility). For binary attribution questions, faithful + stable alone is impossible (the bilemma). The theorem requires zero domain axioms — only the Rashomon property.

The impossibility applies at two granularities. Input-level: 50 retrains produce 24 distinct top-3 SHAP rankings (4.2% agreement); 45% of German Credit applicants receive different explanation categories; 68% of 77 datasets show instability (>10% within-group flip rate). Component-level: 10 independently trained transformers on TinyStories agree at only rho=0.54 on circuit importance by activation patching. A G-invariant projection (orbit averaging over the architectural symmetry group) lifts agreement to rho=0.98, with 14/14 theory-derived predictions confirmed across two scales. A fine-tuned GPT-2 confirms the boundary: without Rashomon diversity, instability vanishes (rho=0.993).

The design space has exactly two achievable families among deterministic methods; DASH (orbit averaging) is Pareto-optimal. A 7-line nonparametric diagnostic outperforms the Gaussian formula by 2x. Verified in Lean 4 (357 theorems/lemmas, 6 axioms, 0 sorry).
